Insects

About Insects

Insects are the most diverse group of animals on Earth, with over a million known species. They have a three-part body structure consisting of the head, thorax, and abdomen. Insects are characterized by having six legs, antennae, and often wings. They have an exoskeleton made of chitin, which provides structure and protection. They play vital roles in ecosystems as pollinators, decomposers, and prey for other animals.

Key Features

  • Cold-blooded (Ectothermic)
  • Three-part body
  • Six legs
  • Exoskeleton
  • Wings
  • Compound Eyes
